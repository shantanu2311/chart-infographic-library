# Infographic & Poster

## What It Is
A large-format, visually rich design approach optimized for print, exhibition, and at-a-glance comprehension. Content is organized into a strong vertical or modular hierarchy where icons, illustrations, and color blocks carry as much informational weight as the data itself. The reader should grasp the main message from three meters away, and discover supporting details up close. This style bridges the gap between a chart and a piece of graphic design — it exists to be looked at, understood, and remembered. Every element fights for limited physical or screen space, so information density must be balanced against visual clarity.

## Key Characteristics
- Clear visual hierarchy with a dominant headline zone, supporting data sections, and a footer/source block
- Icon-heavy: pictograms, custom icons, and illustrated symbols replace or augment abstract chart elements
- Bold color blocking — large areas of flat color define sections and guide the eye through the layout
- Print-ready resolution (300 DPI minimum) with CMYK color consideration for physical output
- Modular grid layout allowing sections to be rearranged for different formats (A1 poster, A4 factsheet, social tile)
- Strong brand integration: logo placement, institutional colors, and consistent footer formatting

## Typography
- **Headline**: Impact display — Bebas Neue, Oswald, Montserrat Black, or Anton at 48-72px (poster) / 28-36px (factsheet) — must read from distance
- **Subheads**: Same family or complementary sans at 20-28px, semibold, with ample top margin
- **Body**: Readable sans — Open Sans, Lato, or Noto Sans at 12-14px (print) / 14-16px (digital), regular weight
- **Data Callouts**: Large numerals at 36-60px in the accent color, with a small unit label beneath
- **Source/Footer**: 8-9px, grey or reversed white on dark footer bar, with logos and attribution

## Chart Types That Work Well
- [[Pictogram Chart]] — icons representing units (one solar panel icon = 1 GW installed); instantly scannable from distance
- [[Proportional Area Chart]] — circles or squares scaled by value; visual impact of relative magnitude
- [[Isotype Chart]] — rows of repeated symbols showing ratios and proportions; Otto Neurath's legacy
- [[Sankey Diagram]] — energy flow visualizations from source to end use; striking when given full poster width
- [[Map Chart / Choropleth]] — geographic data displayed at large format with rich regional detail

## Design Tips
> [!tip] Best Practices
> - Design at actual print size from the start — an A1 poster designed at A4 and scaled up will look amateur
> - Apply the "3-30-300" rule: the headline should communicate from 3 meters, key data from 30 centimeters, detail text from reading distance
> - Limit to 3 main sections or data stories per poster — more than that creates visual chaos
> - Use icon sets consistently: if you use a filled style for one icon, all icons should be filled (do not mix line and filled styles)
> - Always export both RGB (digital/screen) and CMYK (print) versions with appropriate bleed margins (3-5mm)
