# Corporate & Business

## What It Is
The visual language of management consulting, investor relations, and enterprise reporting. Every element serves a purpose within a rigid brand system. Charts are designed to be understood in a boardroom at a glance, projected on screens, and printed in annual reports. Consistency, professionalism, and absolute clarity of message are paramount — there is no room for ambiguity when millions of dollars or policy decisions ride on interpretation.

## Key Characteristics
- Strict grid system with consistent spacing (8px or 12px base unit)
- Every data element labeled — axis titles, units, source attribution, date ranges
- Brand-aligned color palette applied uniformly across all deliverables
- Clear legend placement (top-left or top-center, never floating inside the chart area)
- Slide-ready proportions (16:9 aspect ratio, 40pt minimum font for projection)
- Footer with source citation, methodology note, and confidentiality marking

## Color Palettes
- **Navy Authority**: `#003366` (primary), `#0066cc` (secondary), `#66b2ff` (tertiary), `#e6f0ff` (background fill), `#cc3333` (alert) — classic consulting
- **Teal Sustainability**: `#006d6f`, `#00a89d`, `#66d9d1`, `#e0f7f6`, `#f4a261` (accent) — ESG and sustainability reporting
- **Slate Professional**: `#2f3e46`, `#52796f`, `#84a98c`, `#cad2c5`, `#354f52` — understated authority
- **Deloitte Green**: `#86bc25` (primary green), `#0076a8` (blue), `#62b5e5` (light blue), `#333333` (text) — strategy house palette

## Typography
- **Headlines**: Geometric sans — Calibri, Segoe UI, Helvetica Neue, or Source Sans Pro at 24-32px, semibold
- **Body**: Same family at 14-16px, regular weight for readability in dense slides
- **Data Labels**: Tabular numerals at 11-13px — Calibri or Arial with proportional spacing disabled
- **Footnotes/Sources**: 9-10px, grey (#666), placed in consistent footer location
- **Avoid**: Serif fonts except in formal annual reports; decorative fonts always

## Best For
- Investor presentations and quarterly earnings decks
- [[MDB]] (Multilateral Development Bank) annual reports and project appraisals
- ESG and sustainability reports following [[GRI]], [[TCFD]], or [[BRSR]] frameworks
- Board-level strategy presentations and C-suite dashboards
- Management consulting deliverables (due diligence, market sizing, benchmarking)

### Energy & Climate Applications
- Scope 1/2/3 emissions breakdowns for corporate disclosure
- Year-over-year emissions intensity trend charts for ESG reporting
- Capital allocation waterfall charts for clean energy investments
- Portfolio carbon footprint analysis for financial institutions
- [[CBAM]] compliance cost projection charts

## Chart Types That Work Well
- [[Stacked Bar Chart]] — scope breakdowns by facility or business unit; clear legend and totals labeled on top
- [[Waterfall Chart]] — bridge charts showing emission reductions from baseline to target; consulting staple
- [[Donut Chart]] — portfolio mix or scope proportions; center label with total value
- [[Grouped Bar Chart]] — year-over-year comparison across categories; consistent color coding
- [[KPI Card]] — headline metric with delta indicator and sparkline; dashboard essential

## Design Tips
> [!tip] Best Practices
> - Establish a chart style guide early: define exact colors, fonts, spacing, and label formats before building any chart
> - Always include units on axes (tCO2e, MWh, INR Cr) — never assume the audience knows the scale
> - Use a maximum of 5-6 colors per chart; beyond that, use small multiples or interactive filters
> - Add a "so what" subtitle under every chart title that states the business implication
> - Test every chart at projected size (1280x720 minimum) to confirm readability from the back of a conference room
