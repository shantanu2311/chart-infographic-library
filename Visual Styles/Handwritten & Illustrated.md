# Handwritten & Illustrated

## What It Is
A deliberately imperfect, human-centered visual approach that strips away the cold precision of digital charts and replaces it with warmth, texture, and personality. Lines wobble. Shapes are organic. Text looks like someone wrote it on a whiteboard. This style lowers the barrier to engagement — people who tune out a bar chart will lean into a sketch. It communicates that data is made by people, for people, and that understanding it does not require a statistics degree.

## Key Characteristics
- Sketch-like strokes with variable line weight and slight irregularity in shapes
- Hand-drawn textures — crosshatching, stippling, watercolor washes, paper grain overlays
- Rounded, imperfect geometry (wobbly circles, uneven bar edges, organic curves)
- Handwritten or hand-lettered typography (or digital fonts that convincingly mimic it)
- Warm, desaturated color palettes evoking craft paper, colored pencils, or watercolors
- Prominent use of icons and small illustrations to supplement or replace abstract data marks

## Color Palettes
- **Craft Paper**: `#f5ebe0` (background), `#d5c4a1` (secondary), `#5c4033` (ink), `#8fbc8f` (green accent), `#cd853f` (warm accent) — stationery warmth
- **Watercolor Earth**: `#e8d5b7`, `#a2c4a5` (sage), `#7eb8c9` (sky), `#d4956a` (terracotta), `#6b4c3b` (sepia ink) — nature documentary feel
- **Colored Pencil**: `#fff8e7` (cream base), `#e76f51` (coral), `#2a9d8f` (teal), `#e9c46a` (gold), `#264653` (deep teal) — vibrant yet soft
- **Chalkboard**: `#2b2b2b` (board), `#ffffff` (chalk), `#ffcb69` (yellow chalk), `#a8dadc` (blue chalk), `#f4845f` (orange chalk) — classroom nostalgia

## Typography
- **Headlines**: Handwritten display — Caveat, Architects Daughter, Amatic SC, or Patrick Hand at 28-40px
- **Body/Labels**: Rounded sans — Nunito, Quicksand, or Comic Neue at 14-16px for legibility
- **Annotations**: Same handwritten face as headlines at 12-14px, slightly rotated for authenticity
- **Numbers**: Keep data values in a clean sans-serif (Nunito) for accuracy — handwritten numbers sacrifice readability
- **Trick**: Mix one handwritten font for emphasis with one clean font for data; never go full handwritten

## Best For
- Educational materials for schools, workshops, and training programs
- Community outreach on climate change basics and local environmental issues
- Social media explainers (Instagram carousels, Twitter threads) about sustainability
- Children's climate content and youth engagement campaigns
- NGO annual reports aimed at donors and general public audiences

### Energy & Climate Applications
- "How does a solar panel work?" illustrated explainers for community adoption campaigns
- Household carbon footprint breakdowns for consumer awareness programs
- Simple before/after sketches showing energy efficiency improvements in homes
- Illustrated guides to waste segregation and circular economy concepts for municipal programs

## Chart Types That Work Well
- [[Pictogram Chart]] — icons replacing abstract bars; a tree icon per 1000 tonnes of CO2 absorbed is instantly understood
- [[Bubble Chart]] — organic circular shapes naturally suit the hand-drawn aesthetic
- [[Area Chart]] — watercolor-fill areas stacked to show composition; layered transparency adds depth
- [[Flowchart / Sankey]] — hand-drawn flow diagrams showing energy or material flows through a system
- [[Icon Array]] — grids of small illustrations representing proportions (e.g., 7 out of 10 factories)

## Design Tips
> [!tip] Best Practices
> - Use Rough.js or a sketch renderer as a final pass over clean SVG charts — this gives the wobble without sacrificing data accuracy
> - Keep the actual data positions precise; only the visual rendering should be imperfect
> - Add small illustrated characters or mascots to guide the reader through complex narratives
> - Layer textures subtly — paper grain at 5-10% opacity, not overwhelming the data
> - Test with the target audience: children and non-experts will tell you immediately if something is confusing

## Tools & Software
- [[Rough.js]] — JavaScript library that renders SVG/Canvas with a hand-drawn, sketchy style
- [[Procreate]] (iPad) — digital illustration with natural brush textures for custom chart art
- [[Figma]] with sketch plugins (e.g., Rough, Hand-drawn) for collaborative design workflows
- [[Excalidraw]] — whiteboard-style drawing tool that exports clean SVGs with a hand-drawn look
