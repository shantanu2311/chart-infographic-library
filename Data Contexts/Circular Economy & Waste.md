# Circular Economy & Waste

## Overview

The circular economy framework aims to decouple economic growth from resource extraction by keeping materials in use through reuse, repair, remanufacturing, and recycling. This domain covers material flow analysis, waste generation and treatment, recycling rates, extended producer responsibility (EPR) schemes, and the embodied carbon embedded in products and supply chains. The global circularity gap remains significant: only about 7-8% of materials entering the economy are cycled back, according to the Circularity Gap Report.

## Key Data Types

- Material flow accounts (extraction, imports, exports, domestic material consumption)
- Waste generation data by source (municipal, industrial, construction, e-waste, plastic)
- Waste treatment pathways (landfill, incineration, recycling, composting, open dump)
- Recycling rates by material stream (plastic, metal, paper, glass, organic, e-waste)
- EPR scheme coverage and collection rates by product category
- Embodied carbon / lifecycle emissions data by material and product
- Circular business model metrics (product-as-a-service, sharing, refurbishment)
- Secondary raw material markets and pricing

## Key Metrics & Indicators

- Circularity gap (% of materials cycled back into the economy)
- Municipal solid waste generation per capita (kg/person/yr)
- Recycling rate by material stream (%)
- Material footprint (tonnes per capita)
- Embodied carbon intensity (kgCO2e per kg of material)
- Plastic waste leakage to environment (Mt/yr)
- E-waste generation and formal collection rate
- Secondary material price index (vs virgin material)

## Primary Data Sources
