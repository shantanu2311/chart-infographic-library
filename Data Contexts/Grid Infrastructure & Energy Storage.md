# Grid Infrastructure & Energy Storage

## Overview

Grid infrastructure and energy storage form the backbone of the energy transition, enabling the integration of variable renewable energy at scale. This domain covers transmission and distribution networks, battery energy storage systems (BESS), pumped hydro, compressed air, and emerging long-duration storage technologies. As renewable penetration rises, grid flexibility, curtailment management, and cross-border interconnection become critical bottlenecks.

## Key Data Types

- Grid capacity data: transmission line length (km), transformer capacity (MVA)
- Energy storage installations by technology (Li-ion, pumped hydro, flow battery, CAES)
- Curtailment volumes (MWh) by region and renewable source
- Grid investment data ($bn) by segment (transmission, distribution, smart grid)
- Flexibility resource inventory: demand response, storage, interconnectors, gas peakers
- Wholesale electricity price and ancillary service price data
- Interconnector utilisation and cross-border flow data
- Renewable energy penetration rates by grid region

## Key Metrics & Indicators

- Battery storage installed capacity (GW) and energy capacity (GWh)
- Annual grid investment ($bn) by category
- Curtailment rate (% of available renewable generation)
- Flexibility gap (GW of needed vs available flexibility resources)
- Storage duration profile (short 1-4 hr vs long-duration 8+ hr)
- Battery storage cost ($/kWh installed, all-in LCOS)
- Transmission congestion cost ($bn/yr)
- Renewable penetration peak instantaneous share (%)

## Primary Data Sources

- IEA — World Energy Investment, Electricity Market Report, Grid and Storage tracking

- LDES Council — Long Duration Energy Storage reports
