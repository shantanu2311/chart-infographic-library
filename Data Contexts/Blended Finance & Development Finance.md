# Blended Finance & Development Finance

## Overview
Tracks public-private blended finance structures that use catalytic capital from development finance institutions (DFIs), multilateral development banks (MDBs), and philanthropies to mobilize private investment into climate and sustainable development projects in emerging markets. Covers first-loss tranches, guarantees, concessional debt, results-based finance, and the mobilization ratios that measure how effectively public money attracts private capital.

## Key Data Types
- Blended finance transaction records (structure, size, participants)
- DFI and MDB climate finance commitments and disbursements
- Mobilization ratios (public to private capital leveraged)
- Instrument-level data (equity, debt, guarantee, grant, results-based)
- Sector and geography allocation breakdowns
- Fund-level performance data (IRR, default rates)

## Key Metrics & Indicators
- Total blended finance mobilized annually (USD bn)
- Private capital mobilization ratio (private USD per public USD)
- DFI/MDB climate finance as share of total portfolio
- Sector allocation (energy, transport, agriculture, water, AFOLU)
- Geographic concentration (LDCs, SIDS, MICs share)
- Average ticket size by instrument type
- First-loss tranche utilization rate
- Results-based payment triggers achieved (%)

## Primary Data Sources
- Convergence — Blended Finance database and annual report
- OECD — Blended Finance and Mobilisation statistics
- World Bank / IFC — Private Capital Mobilization reports
- Climate Policy Initiative (CPI) — Global Landscape of Climate Finance
- MDB Joint Report on Climate Finance
- Green Climate Fund (GCF) — portfolio and mobilization data
- GIIN — Impact Investing Market Size and benchmarks
- Donor Tracker — bilateral DFI commitment data
