# Deforestation & Land Use Change

## Overview
Tracks forest loss, land-use change emissions, and the drivers behind deforestation including commodity agriculture (soy, palm oil, cattle, cocoa), logging, and infrastructure expansion. Land-use change accounts for approximately 11% of global CO2 emissions. Covers REDD+ mechanisms, restoration commitments, peatland degradation, and corporate deforestation-free supply chain pledges under the EU Deforestation Regulation (EUDR).

## Key Data Types
- Tree cover loss and gain (hectares per year, by country and biome)
- Land-use change CO2 emissions (GtCO2/yr)
- Commodity production volumes linked to deforestation risk
- Protected area coverage and effectiveness metrics
- REDD+ credit issuance and retirement volumes
- Peatland area and degradation status
- Fire alert and hotspot data (VIIRS, MODIS)

## Key Metrics & Indicators
- Tree cover loss (Mha/yr) by country and primary driver
- Land-use change emissions (GtCO2/yr)
- Commodity-driven deforestation exposure (ha per tonne produced)
- Forest restoration area committed vs achieved (Mha)
- REDD+ credits issued and retired (MtCO2e)
- Intact forest landscape loss rate
- Peatland emissions (GtCO2e/yr)
- Fire-related tree cover loss as share of total

## Primary Data Sources
- Global Forest Watch (WRI) — near-real-time tree cover loss alerts
- FAO Forest Resources Assessment (FRA) — national forest inventories
- Hansen / University of Maryland — annual tree cover loss dataset
- INPE PRODES / DETER — Brazilian Amazon deforestation monitoring
- Global Forest Watch Pro — commodity risk and supply chain tools
- Trase — commodity trade and deforestation linkage platform
- IPCC AR6 WG III — AFOLU sector emissions
- UNEP — Peatland Atlas and restoration tracking
