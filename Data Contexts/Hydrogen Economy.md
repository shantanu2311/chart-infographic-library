# Hydrogen Economy

## Overview

The hydrogen economy encompasses the production, transport, storage, and end-use of hydrogen as a clean energy carrier and industrial feedstock. Hydrogen is colour-coded by production pathway: grey (unabated fossil), blue (fossil + CCS), and green (electrolysis powered by renewables). Global demand stands at roughly 95 Mt/yr, overwhelmingly grey, with green and blue projects scaling rapidly as electrolyzer costs fall and policy support strengthens.

## Key Data Types

- Production volumes by pathway (grey, blue, green, pink/nuclear)
- Electrolyzer installed and pipeline capacity (MW/GW)
- Hydrogen demand by end-use sector (refining, ammonia, steel, transport, power)
- Infrastructure data: pipelines, refuelling stations, storage caverns
- Project-level FID status, capacity, and CAPEX
- Levelised cost of hydrogen (LCOH) by pathway and region
- Trade flow projections (export/import corridors)

## Key Metrics & Indicators

- Green hydrogen production cost ($/kg H2)
- Electrolyzer installed capacity (GW) and annual additions
- Total hydrogen demand (Mt/yr) by sector
- Project pipeline by FID status (announced, FID, operational)
- Blue hydrogen capture rate (% of CO2 captured)
- Electrolyzer utilisation / capacity factor (%)
- Hydrogen blend ratio in gas networks (vol %)
- Announced investment ($bn) by region
