# Article 6 & International Carbon Trading

## Overview
Covers the Paris Agreement's Article 6 mechanisms for international carbon market cooperation. Article 6.2 enables bilateral and multilateral trading of Internationally Transferred Mitigation Outcomes (ITMOs) between countries. Article 6.4 establishes a centralized crediting mechanism (successor to the CDM). Tracks the operationalization of corresponding adjustments, host country approvals, methodology development, and the emerging architecture of compliance-grade international carbon trading.

## Key Data Types
- ITMO transaction records (buyer, seller, volume, vintage, price)
- Bilateral agreement registries and terms
- Article 6.4 methodology submissions and approvals
- Host country authorization letters and corresponding adjustment registers
- NDC ambition levels and conditional targets linked to carbon market revenue
- Registry infrastructure status by country

## Key Metrics & Indicators
- ITMO transactions completed (volume in MtCO2e and value in USD)
- Number of bilateral Article 6.2 agreements signed
- Corresponding adjustment implementation status by country
- Article 6.4 approved methodologies count
- Host country authorization letters issued
- Price discovery for ITMOs (USD/tCO2e) across bilateral deals
- Share of NDC targets conditional on market mechanisms
- Registry readiness index by country

## Primary Data Sources
- UNFCCC — Article 6 negotiations, rulebook, and registry
