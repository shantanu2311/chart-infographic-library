# Loss & Damage Finance

## Overview
Covers the financing mechanisms for addressing climate-related loss and damage that goes beyond what adaptation can prevent. Includes the Warsaw International Mechanism (WIM), the Loss and Damage Fund established at COP28, attribution science linking extreme events to climate change, and the distinction between slow-onset events (sea-level rise, desertification) and sudden events (cyclones, floods). Tracks pledges, disbursements, insurance gaps, and the political economy of climate liability.

## Key Data Types
- Economic losses from climate-related disasters (insured and uninsured)
- Loss and Damage Fund pledge and disbursement records
- Attribution study results (fraction of risk attributable to climate change)
- Vulnerability indices by country and community
- Insurance penetration and protection gap data
- Slow-onset event impact assessments (sea-level rise, salinization, glacier retreat)

## Key Metrics & Indicators
- Total economic losses from climate events (USD bn/yr)
- Insured vs uninsured loss gap (USD bn)
- L&D Fund pledges vs actual disbursements (USD)
- Climate attribution fraction for major events (%)
- Vulnerability indices (V20, ND-GAIN, Germanwatch CRI rank)
- Number of people displaced by climate events (millions/yr)
- Slow-onset loss valuation estimates
- Insurance penetration rate in climate-vulnerable countries (%)

## Primary Data Sources
- UNFCCC Warsaw International Mechanism (WIM) and Santiago Network
- V20 (Vulnerable Twenty) Group of Finance Ministers
- Germanwatch — Global Climate Risk Index (CRI)
- EM-DAT / CRED — International Disaster Database
- Munich Re / Swiss Re — NatCatSERVICE and Sigma reports
- World Weather Attribution — event attribution studies
- Loss and Damage Collaboration (L&DC)
- IPCC AR6 WG II — losses, damages, and limits to adaptation
