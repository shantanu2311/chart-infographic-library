# Just Transition & Social Impact

## Overview
Tracks the socioeconomic consequences of shifting from fossil fuel-dependent economies to clean energy systems. Covers employment displacement and creation, community-level impacts in coal and oil regions, retraining and reskilling programmes, energy poverty, and distributional equity across income groups, geographies, and demographics. Central to ensuring climate action does not deepen inequality.

## Key Data Types
- Employment statistics (jobs lost, created, transitioned) by sector and region
- Retraining programme enrolment, completion, and placement rates
- Household energy expenditure and affordability indices
- Community economic output in fossil-dependent regions
- Social protection coverage and transfer payments
- Demographic breakdowns (gender, age, education level)

## Key Metrics & Indicators
- Fossil fuel jobs at risk of displacement (millions)
- Green jobs created per year by sector and geography
- Retraining investment per displaced worker (USD)
- Energy poverty rate (% of households spending >10% income on energy)
- Gini coefficient change in transition regions
- Median wage differential (fossil vs clean energy roles)
- Social protection coverage rate in affected communities
- Community economic resilience index

## Primary Data Sources
- International Labour Organization (ILO) — World Employment and Social Outlook
- World Bank — Just Transition country diagnostics
- IRENA — Renewable Energy and Jobs Annual Review
- Just Transition Centre / ITUC
- Climate Investment Funds (CIF) — Just Transition portfolio
- OECD — Employment Outlook and regional transition reports
- National statistical offices (coal region employment data)
- European Commission — Territorial Just Transition Plans
