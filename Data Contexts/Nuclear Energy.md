# Nuclear Energy

## Overview
Covers the global nuclear power fleet, new reactor construction programmes (Generation III+ and small modular reactors), uranium fuel markets, spent fuel management and decommissioning, and the role of nuclear in net-zero energy systems as a firm low-carbon baseload source. Includes public acceptance dynamics, regulatory timelines, and the economics of nuclear versus competing low-carbon technologies.

## Key Data Types
- Reactor fleet data (operational, under construction, planned, decommissioned)
- Capacity factors and generation output (TWh/yr)
- Uranium production, trade, enrichment, and stockpile data
- Construction timelines and cost overruns for new builds
- SMR design certification and deployment pipeline
- Decommissioning cost estimates and waste inventory volumes
- Public opinion survey data on nuclear energy

## Key Metrics & Indicators
- Global installed nuclear capacity (GWe)
- Fleet average capacity factor (%)
- New build pipeline (units under construction and planned)
- Uranium spot price (USD/lb U3O8)
- Levelized cost of electricity (LCOE) for nuclear vs alternatives (USD/MWh)
- Spent fuel and high-level waste volume (m3)
- Construction duration (months from first concrete to grid connection)
- SMR designs in regulatory review or licensed

## Primary Data Sources
- IAEA PRIS (Power Reactor Information System) — global reactor database
- World Nuclear Association (WNA) — reactor database and country profiles
- NEA/OECD — Nuclear Energy Data and projected costs of electricity
- UxC — Uranium market data and enrichment prices
- US NRC / UK ONR / CNSC — regulatory filings and SMR certifications
- IEA — Nuclear Power in a Clean Energy System and World Energy Outlook
- WANO — fleet performance indicators
- Eurobarometer / Gallup — public acceptance polling
