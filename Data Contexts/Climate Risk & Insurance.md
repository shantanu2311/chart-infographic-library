# Climate Risk & Insurance

## Overview

Climate risk spans physical risks (acute events like floods and storms, chronic shifts like sea-level rise and heat stress), transition risks (policy, technology, and market shifts toward low-carbon), and liability/litigation risks. The insurance industry sits at the intersection, pricing and absorbing climate-related losses while facing growing protection gaps globally. Central banks and financial regulators increasingly mandate climate stress testing and scenario analysis aligned with NGFS and TCFD frameworks.

## Key Data Types

- Natural catastrophe loss data (insured, economic, by peril and geography)
- Climate stress test scenarios and results (NGFS orderly, disorderly, hot house)
- Physical risk exposure metrics (value-at-risk under warming scenarios)
- Transition risk indicators (stranded asset estimates, carbon price pathways)
- Insurance market data: premiums, combined ratios, reinsurance pricing
- Climate litigation case databases (counts, jurisdictions, outcomes)
- Credit rating climate risk overlays and sovereign risk assessments
- TCFD/ISSB disclosure compliance rates and quality scores

## Key Metrics & Indicators

- Annual insured vs total economic natural catastrophe losses ($bn)
- Protection gap: uninsured share of total losses (%)
- Value-at-Risk (VaR) under NGFS climate scenarios
- Combined ratio trends for property catastrophe lines
- Stranded asset exposure ($bn) in fossil fuel portfolios
- Climate litigation case count (cumulative and annual filings)
- Climate stress test capital impact (bps of CET1 ratio)
- TCFD-aligned disclosure rate among listed companies (%)

## Primary Data Sources

- Network for Greening the Financial System (NGFS) — Climate scenarios, supervisory expectations
- Swiss Re Institute — Sigma reports, NatCatSERVICE
- Munich Re — NatCatSERVICE, Topics Geo annual review
- Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures (TCFD) — Status reports
- European Central Bank (ECB) — Climate stress test results, risk dashboard
- Bank of England — Climate Biennial Exploratory Scenario (CBES) results
- Grantham Research Institute (LSE) — Climate Change Laws of the World, litigation database
- S&P Global / Moody's — Climate credit risk assessments, physical risk scores
