# Geopolitics & Energy Security

## Overview

Geopolitics and energy security examines how energy supply, demand, and trade shape international relations, economic stability, and strategic competition. This domain covers energy import dependence, strategic petroleum reserves, critical mineral supply chains, trade route chokepoints, sanctions regimes, and resource nationalism. The energy transition is reshaping these dynamics as dependency shifts from fossil fuels to critical minerals (lithium, cobalt, rare earths) concentrated in a small number of producing countries.

## Key Data Types

- Energy trade flow data (oil, gas, coal, LNG) by origin and destination
- Import dependency ratios by country and fuel type
- Strategic petroleum reserve levels (days of import cover)
- Critical mineral production, reserves, and refining concentration data
- Trade route and chokepoint throughput (Strait of Hormuz, Malacca, Suez)
- Sanctions and trade restriction registries
- Energy infrastructure data: pipelines, LNG terminals, refineries
- Geopolitical risk indices and country risk ratings

## Key Metrics & Indicators

- Energy import dependency (% of primary energy from imports)
- Strategic reserve coverage (days of net imports)
- Supply concentration index (Herfindahl-Hirschman Index) for fuels and minerals
- Trade flow volumes (Mtoe, bcm, Mt) by corridor
- Chokepoint throughput (million barrels/day or bcm/yr)
- Critical mineral refining share by country (% of global)
- LNG spot price differentials across hubs (JKM vs TTF vs Henry Hub)
- Geopolitical Risk Index (GPR) and energy-specific sub-indices

## Primary Data Sources

- IEA — World Energy Outlook, Energy Security section, Critical Minerals Outlook
- US Energy Information Administration (EIA) — International Energy Statistics, Country Analysis Briefs
- Chatham House — Resource Trade Earth, energy and geopolitics programmes
- Oxford Institute for Energy Studies (OIES) — Gas, oil, and energy transition papers
- Brookings Institution — Energy Security and Climate Initiative
- BP Statistical Review of World Energy (now Energy Institute)
- USGS — Mineral Commodity Summaries, critical mineral assessments
- European Commission — Critical Raw Materials Act, supply chain monitoring
