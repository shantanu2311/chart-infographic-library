# Green Bonds & Climate Finance Instruments

## Overview

Green, social, sustainability, and sustainability-linked (GSSS) bonds are fixed-income instruments whose proceeds are earmarked for climate and environmental projects. The market has grown from under $50 billion in annual issuance (2015) to over $900 billion (2024), with green bonds comprising the largest share. Transition bonds and blended climate funds extend the universe to hard-to-abate sectors and emerging-market deployment.

## Key Data Types

- Bond-level issuance records (ISIN, issuer, currency, tenor, coupon, label)
- Use-of-proceeds allocation (energy, transport, water, buildings, land use)
- Secondary market pricing and spread data (greenium)
- Fund-level AUM, deployment, and vintage data
- Sovereign vs corporate vs supranational issuer classification
- Verification and second-party opinion (SPO) status
- Alignment with ICMA Green Bond Principles or Climate Bonds Standard

## Key Metrics & Indicators

- Annual and cumulative GSSS issuance volume ($bn)
- Greenium (yield spread vs conventional bonds, bps)
- Use-of-proceeds allocation breakdown (% by category)
- Geographic distribution of issuance (by country/region)
- Issuer-type split (sovereign, corporate, financial, supranational)
- Currency denomination breakdown
- Average tenor and coupon rate
- Share of sustainability-linked vs use-of-proceeds structures

## Primary Data Sources

- Climate Bonds Initiative (CBI) — Market Intelligence reports, Green Bond Database
- International Capital Market Association (ICMA) — Green Bond Principles, Social Bond Principles
- Bloomberg Terminal — BNEF Green Bond Index, BGREEN screen
- Environmental Finance — Bond database, league tables
- Refinitiv / LSEG — Sustainable finance data feed
- World Bank Treasury — Sovereign green bond tracker
- OECD — Green bond mobilisation estimates
- Amundi-IFC Emerging Market Green Bond Fund reports
