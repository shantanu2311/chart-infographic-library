# Commodity Markets (Oil Gas Metals)

## Overview
Covers the pricing, trading, supply-demand dynamics, and geopolitical factors driving global commodity markets for energy (crude oil, natural gas, LNG, coal) and metals (copper, aluminum, nickel, lithium, iron ore, precious metals). These markets are foundational to understanding energy transition economics, industrial decarbonization costs, and the resource requirements for clean energy infrastructure buildout.

## Key Data Types
- Spot and futures prices (daily, weekly, monthly)
- Production and consumption volumes by country
- Inventory and stockpile levels (commercial and strategic)
- Trade flow and shipping data (tanker tracking, port throughput)
- OPEC+ production quotas and compliance rates
- Exchange-traded volumes and open interest (LME, NYMEX, ICE)
- Cost curves and marginal production costs

## Key Metrics & Indicators
- Brent/WTI crude oil spread (USD/bbl)
- Henry Hub and TTF natural gas prices (USD/MMBtu, EUR/MWh)
- LNG spot price (JKM marker, USD/MMBtu)
- Coal prices API2 (Europe) and API4 (South Africa) (USD/t)
- Copper and aluminum LME prices (USD/t)
- Lithium carbonate and hydroxide prices (USD/t)
- OPEC+ quota compliance rate (%)
- Days of forward demand cover (crude oil inventories)

## Primary Data Sources
- S&P Global Platts — commodity price assessments and analytics
- Argus Media — price reporting and market intelligence
- London Metal Exchange (LME) — base metals pricing and warehousing
- NYMEX / CME Group — energy and metals futures
- ICE — Brent crude, gas, and power futures
- EIA (US Energy Information Administration) — production, consumption, inventory data
- OPEC — Monthly Oil Market Report and production data
- World Bureau of Metal Statistics (WBMS)
