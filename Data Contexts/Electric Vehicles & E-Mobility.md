# Electric Vehicles & E-Mobility

## Overview

The electric vehicle sector covers battery electric vehicles (BEV), plug-in hybrids (PHEV), electric two- and three-wheelers, electric buses, and commercial trucks. Global EV sales exceeded 17 million units in 2024, representing roughly 20% of new car sales. The sector is shaped by battery cost trajectories, charging infrastructure rollout, and policy mandates such as ICE phase-out dates and emission standards.

## Key Data Types

- Vehicle sales data by powertrain (BEV, PHEV, FCEV), segment, and market
- Battery pack and cell-level cost data ($/kWh at pack level)
- Charging infrastructure: public and private charger counts by power level (AC L2, DC fast)
- Fleet composition and stock data (cumulative EVs on road)
- Battery chemistry market share (NMC, LFP, solid-state pipeline)
- OEM production and order backlog data
- Total cost of ownership (TCO) comparison models
- Policy tracker: subsidies, mandates, emission zones

## Key Metrics & Indicators

- EV sales share of new vehicle sales (%)
- Battery pack cost ($/kWh, weighted average)
- Public charging points per EV ratio
- Cumulative EV fleet size (millions)
- Fleet TCO parity year (BEV vs ICE, by segment)
- Average EV range (km/miles) by segment
- DC fast charger deployment rate (units/quarter)
- Battery energy density (Wh/kg at cell level)

## Primary Data Sources

- IEA Global EV Outlook (GEVO) — Sales, stock, policy, infrastructure data
- BloombergNEF (BNEF) — Battery Price Survey, EV Outlook, charging tracker
- EV-Volumes — Monthly global EV sales tracker by model and market
- Rho Motion — Battery and charging market intelligence
- China Association of Automobile Manufacturers (CAAM) — Chinese market data
- European Automobile Manufacturers' Association (ACEA) — EU registration data
- US DOE Alternative Fuels Station Locator — US charging infrastructure
- VAHAN / FAME India dashboard — Indian EV registration data
