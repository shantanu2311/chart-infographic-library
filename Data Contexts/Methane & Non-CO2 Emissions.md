# Methane & Non-CO2 Emissions

## Overview
Covers methane (CH4) from oil and gas operations, agriculture, landfills, and coal mining, as well as nitrous oxide (N2O), hydrofluorocarbons (HFCs), sulfur hexafluoride (SF6), and other non-CO2 greenhouse gases. Methane is responsible for roughly 30% of global warming since pre-industrial times, and its short atmospheric lifetime makes it a high-leverage near-term mitigation target. The Global Methane Pledge and satellite-based monitoring are transforming accountability.

## Key Data Types
- Methane emission inventories by sector and country (Mt CH4/yr)
- Satellite-detected super-emitter events (plume data)
- Leak detection and repair (LDAR) survey results
- HFC consumption and production under Kigali Amendment
- Agricultural emission factors (enteric fermentation, rice paddies, manure)
- Waste sector methane generation and capture rates

## Key Metrics & Indicators
- Global methane emissions (Mt CH4/yr) by sector
- Oil and gas methane leak rate (% of production)
- Abatement potential by technology and cost tier (Mt CH4/yr)
- GWP-weighted totals (Mt CO2e) using AR5 and AR6 values
- Satellite-detected large emission events per quarter
- HFC phase-down progress (% of Kigali baseline)
- Landfill gas capture rate (%)
- N2O emissions from fertiliser application (kt N2O/yr)

## Primary Data Sources
- IEA Methane Tracker — Global methane emissions from energy
- Global Methane Initiative (GMI)
- UNEP International Methane Emissions Observatory (IMEO)
- US EPA — Greenhouse Gas Inventory and Global Non-CO2 projections
- NOAA Global Monitoring Laboratory — atmospheric CH4 concentrations
- Carbon Mapper / MethaneSAT / GHGSat — satellite plume data
- IPCC AR6 WG III — non-CO2 mitigation chapters
- FAO GLEAM — livestock methane modelling
