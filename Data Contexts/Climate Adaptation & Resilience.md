# Climate Adaptation & Resilience

## Overview

Climate adaptation and resilience covers the strategies, investments, and metrics for preparing societies and economies for the physical impacts of climate change. This includes adaptation finance flows, climate vulnerability indices, infrastructure resilience, early warning systems, and the growing "adaptation gap" between what is needed and what is being invested. The domain intersects heavily with disaster risk reduction, insurance markets, and development finance.

## Key Data Types

- Adaptation finance flows ($bn) by source, channel, and recipient
- Climate vulnerability and readiness indices (ND-GAIN, INFORM)
- Natural disaster loss data (economic, insured, fatalities)
- Physical climate risk metrics (heat stress days, flood frequency, sea-level rise projections)
- Infrastructure resilience assessments and investment needs
- National Adaptation Plan (NAP) status and implementation progress
- Early warning system coverage data
- Climate migration and displacement statistics

## Key Metrics & Indicators

- Annual adaptation finance ($bn) and estimated adaptation needs gap
- ND-GAIN Country Index (vulnerability vs readiness scores)
- Annual economic losses from weather-related disasters ($bn)
- Protection gap (uninsured % of total disaster losses)
- Population exposed to climate hazards (millions, by hazard type)
- Countries with National Adaptation Plans submitted
- Early warning system coverage (% of population)
- Climate-resilient infrastructure investment as % of total infrastructure spend

## Primary Data Sources

- Global Center on Adaptation (GCA) — State and Trends in Adaptation report
- UNEP — Adaptation Gap Report (annual)
- Notre Dame Global Adaptation Initiative (ND-GAIN) — Country Index
- Swiss Re Institute — Sigma natural catastrophe database
- Munich Re — NatCatSERVICE loss database
- OECD — Adaptation-related development finance tracking (Rio markers)
- World Bank — Climate Change Action Plan, climate finance data
- IPCC — AR6 WGII impacts, adaptation, and vulnerability assessments
