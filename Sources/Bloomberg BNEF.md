# Bloomberg BNEF

## Overview
Bloomberg New Energy Finance (BNEF) is the premium research arm of Bloomberg LP focused on clean energy, advanced transport, digital industry, innovative materials, and commodities. Their analysis drives billions in investment decisions, combining financial rigor with deep energy sector expertise. BNEF's New Energy Outlook is the definitive investment-grade long-term energy scenario.

## Signature Visual Style
- **Color palette**: Bloomberg terminal aesthetic — dark backgrounds (#1E1E1E), bright accent colors for data series. Signature Bloomberg blue (#2800D7), green for clean energy, orange for fossil. High contrast for readability on dark backgrounds
- **Typography**: Bloomberg proprietary sans-serif, monospaced numerals, dense data tables with minimal decoration. Financial terminal influence throughout
- **Layout**: Dashboard-dense with multiple small charts per page. Emphasis on data tables alongside charts. Slide-deck-optimized formatting for institutional clients
- **Tone**: Corporate, investment-focused, deliberately premium and exclusive. Numbers-first, narrative-second

## Strengths
- Investment-grade data quality with financial modeling rigor applied to energy analysis
- Unmatched coverage of clean energy investment flows — deal-level tracking globally
- Cost learning curve analysis that has accurately predicted solar and battery price declines
- Integrated financial and technical analysis (LCOE decomposition includes financing costs)
- Real-time data feeds integrated with Bloomberg Terminal ecosystem

## Chart Types They Favor
- [[Line Chart]] — cost trajectories, deployment curves, investment trends over time
- [[Stacked Area Chart]] — investment by sector/region, capacity additions by technology
- [[Waterfall Chart]] — LCOE cost decomposition showing capital, O&M, fuel, and financing components
- [[Bubble Chart]] — investment landscape mapping (x=maturity, y=return, size=deal volume)
- [[Candlestick Chart]] — commodity and carbon price tracking in financial chart format

## Design Patterns Worth Studying
- **Cost learning curves**: Log-log plots of cost vs cumulative deployment, extrapolating future prices from historical experience rates
- **Investment flow tracking**: Sankey-style flows from investor type to technology to geography
- **Technology S-curves**: Sigmoid adoption curves with current position marked and forecast confidence bands
- **Dark-mode terminal design**: High-information-density layouts optimized for professional analysis on dark backgrounds

## Energy & Climate Relevance
BNEF is the bridge between climate science and capital markets. Their analysis directly influences where clean energy investment flows, making them critical for understanding the financial viability of emission reduction technologies. Their LCOE data is essential for any cost-benefit analysis of decarbonization strategies.

## Key Reports / Products
- **New Energy Outlook (NEO)** — Annual long-term scenario model for global energy system to 2050, investment-grade
- **Energy Transition Investment Trends** — Annual tracker of global clean energy investment by sector and region
- **Battery Price Survey** — Annual benchmark of lithium-ion battery pack prices (the most cited number in EV economics)
- **Climatescope** — Emerging market clean energy investment climate ranking
- **BNEF Terminal** — Real-time data platform integrated with Bloomberg Terminal

## Data Access
- Paid subscription required for full access (institutional pricing, typically $20K+/year)
- Selected headline figures released publicly in press releases and blog posts
- Climatescope data freely available for emerging market analysis
- No public API; data delivered through Bloomberg Terminal or BNEF platform
- Academic access programs available through some university licenses
