# Reuters Graphics

## Overview
Reuters Graphics is the visual journalism team at Reuters, one of the world's largest news agencies. They produce interactive data stories, explanatory graphics, and real-time data dashboards covering breaking news, climate, finance, and geopolitics. Their work combines wire-service speed with digital-native interactive design, reaching audiences through Reuters and syndicated to thousands of media outlets globally.

## Signature Visual Style
- **Color palette**: Clean, neutral base (whites, light greys) with selective use of Reuters orange (#FF8000) for brand accent. Data colors drawn from accessible, muted palettes. Sequential blues and diverging red-blue scales common
- **Typography**: Reuters proprietary sans-serif, clear and utilitarian. Headline-led with concise subheads. Annotation text kept tight and scannable
- **Layout**: Scrollytelling format dominates — vertical narrative flow with charts that animate or transform as the reader scrolls. Mobile-first responsive design
- **Tone**: Journalistic, objective, explanatory. Lets data speak with minimal editorial overlay

## Strengths
- Exceptional scrollytelling execution — among the best in the industry at scroll-driven narrative
- Speed of production: can produce polished interactive graphics on breaking news within hours
- Strong annotation practice with contextual callouts that guide readers through complex data
- Sophisticated before/after and step-by-step reveal techniques
- Syndication model means their graphics reach massive global audiences through partner outlets

## Chart Types They Favor
- [[Line Chart]] — time series with heavy annotation, scroll-triggered highlighting of key moments
- [[Annotated Bar Chart]] — rankings and comparisons with contextual labels and reference lines
- [[Choropleth Map]] — geographic stories with interactive hover details and zoom capability
- [[Scrollytelling]] — the dominant format: narrative text triggers chart transitions as reader scrolls
- [[Small Multiples]] — grid comparisons of countries or categories that build up through scroll progression

## Design Patterns Worth Studying
- **Step-by-step scrolling narratives**: Charts that build incrementally — each scroll step adds a data layer, annotation, or comparison, controlling the reader's attention
- **Before/after comparisons**: Side-by-side or overlay transitions showing change over time, often with slider interaction
- **Interactive exploration zones**: Embedded within longer narratives, sections where the reader can freely explore data before the narrative resumes
- **Real-time data dashboards**: Live-updating visualizations for elections, markets, and climate events with auto-refresh

## Energy & Climate Relevance
Reuters Graphics produces extensive climate and energy coverage including Arctic ice tracking, extreme weather visualization, emissions trajectory analysis, and energy market reporting. Their climate stories often combine satellite data, scientific datasets, and economic figures into cohesive interactive narratives that make climate impacts tangible.

## Key Reports / Products
- **Reuters Graphics interactive stories** — Standalone scrollytelling pieces published throughout the year on major topics
- **Climate tracker** — Ongoing interactive coverage of global temperature, ice, and emissions
- **Election dashboards** — Real-time results visualization (methodology transferable to any live-data context)
- **Wider Image** — Photo-led stories with data overlays combining photojournalism and visualization
- **Graphics archive** — Searchable collection of all published interactive and static graphics

## Data Access
- Interactive graphics freely viewable on Reuters website
- No raw data downloads; they visualize third-party and proprietary Reuters data
- Graphics syndicated to partner media outlets under licensing agreements
- Methodology notes included in major interactive pieces
- Source citations on every graphic linking to underlying datasets
