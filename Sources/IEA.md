# IEA

## Overview
The International Energy Agency is the world's foremost authority on energy analysis, providing governments with data, policy recommendations, and scenario modelling across all energy sectors. Founded in 1974 after the oil crisis, the IEA has evolved into the central reference point for global energy transitions, with its World Energy Outlook shaping policy worldwide.

## Signature Visual Style
- **Color palette**: Muted corporate blues, greys, and teals. Scenario-specific colors: STEPS (blue), APS (teal), NZE (green). Accent orange for highlights
- **Typography**: Clean sans-serif throughout, moderate hierarchy, dense but legible data labels
- **Layout**: Report-centric with static charts designed for PDF and slide decks. Dashboard-style technology tracking pages online
- **Tone**: Authoritative, policy-neutral, deliberately measured and institutional

## Strengths
- Gold-standard energy balances covering every country and fuel type since 1971
- Scenario modelling framework (STEPS, APS, NZE) that defines the global conversation on energy futures
- Comprehensive technology tracking across 500+ clean energy indicators
- Deep integration of energy economics with physical energy flow data
- Trusted equally by governments, industry, and civil society

## Chart Types They Favor
- [[Stacked Area Chart]] — energy supply/demand by source over time, the backbone of WEO projections
- [[Sankey Diagram]] — energy balance flows from primary supply through transformation to final consumption
- [[Line Chart]] — scenario pathway comparisons showing STEPS vs APS vs NZE trajectories
- [[Choropleth Map]] — geographic distribution of energy access, investment, or emissions
- [[Waterfall Chart]] — decomposing emissions changes or cost structures into component drivers

## Design Patterns Worth Studying
- **Three-scenario comparison**: Every projection shown across STEPS (current policies), APS (announced pledges), and NZE (net zero by 2050) — creating implicit policy pressure
- **Technology tracking dashboard**: Stoplight rating system (on track / needs attention / off track) for each clean energy technology
- **Energy balance Sankeys**: Comprehensive primary-to-final energy flow diagrams that reveal system-level inefficiencies
- **Wedge analysis**: Stacked area showing which technologies contribute what share of emissions reductions between scenarios

## Energy & Climate Relevance
The IEA is the single most influential institution in energy and climate data. Their World Energy Outlook scenarios define what "on track" means for climate targets. Their energy balances underpin virtually all national GHG inventories, and their emission factors are reference standards for Scope 1 and Scope 2 calculations.

## Key Reports / Products
- **World Energy Outlook (WEO)** — Annual flagship: 400+ page scenario analysis of global energy to 2050
- **Net Zero by 2050 Roadmap** — Sector-by-sector pathway to net zero emissions
- **Tracking Clean Energy Progress** — Annual scorecard for 50+ clean energy technologies
- **Energy Technology Perspectives** — Deep dive on technology innovation and deployment
- **World Energy Balances** — Comprehensive energy supply and consumption data for 150+ countries

## Data Access
- Free data portal with selected indicators and interactive charts
- Paid subscription for full datasets (IEA Data & Statistics)
- Bulk data licensing available for institutions
- API access included with paid subscriptions
- Some flagship datasets now released free under open license
