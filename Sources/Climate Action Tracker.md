# Climate Action Tracker

## Overview
Climate Action Tracker (CAT) is an independent scientific project that tracks government climate action and measures it against the Paris Agreement goals. Run by Climate Analytics and NewClimate Institute, CAT provides the most widely cited assessment of whether national climate pledges (NDCs) are sufficient to limit warming to 1.5C or 2C. Their traffic-light rating system has become the de facto standard for climate policy assessment.

## Signature Visual Style
- **Color palette**: Traffic-light rating system that defines their visual identity. 1.5C compatible=#2CA02C (green), 2C compatible=#66BB6A, insufficient=#FFC107 (amber), highly insufficient=#FF9800 (orange), critically insufficient=#F44336 (red). Dark grey backgrounds for warming projection charts
- **Typography**: Clean sans-serif throughout, moderate weight. Rating labels in bold uppercase. Dense annotation on projection charts
- **Layout**: Country scorecard format with rating badge prominently displayed. Warming thermometer visualizations. Projection charts with multiple scenario lines
- **Tone**: Scientific but advocacy-adjacent, creating urgency through data. Deliberately confrontational ratings designed to pressure governments

## Strengths
- The most recognized and trusted system for rating national climate ambition
- Clear, intuitive traffic-light encoding that communicates instantly without explanation
- Rigorous methodology linking national policies to implied global temperature outcomes
- Regular updates around COP conferences creating media moments and policy pressure
- Country-level granularity with consistent cross-country comparability

## Chart Types They Favor
- [[Thermometer Chart]] — warming projections shown as thermometer gauges with 1.5C and 2C thresholds marked
- [[Bar Chart]] — country ratings displayed as color-coded horizontal bars sorted by ambition level
- [[Line Chart]] — emissions pathway projections under current policies vs NDC vs 1.5C-compatible scenarios
- [[Counterfactual Chart]] — showing what global temperature would be if all countries matched a given country's ambition level

## Design Patterns Worth Studying
- **Traffic-light rating system**: 5-tier color-coded assessment (critically insufficient to 1.5C compatible) that has become the global standard for climate policy communication
- **Warming stripe variant**: Using their rating colors in strip format to show how country ambitions translate to warming outcomes
- **NDC assessment scorecards**: Standardized country profiles with rating badge, key metrics, policy summary, and emissions trajectory — a template for any policy evaluation
- **Global temperature projection aggregation**: Combining individual country assessments into aggregate warming projections with uncertainty ranges

## Energy & Climate Relevance
CAT is directly relevant to any organization setting science-based targets or assessing climate policy risk. Their ratings contextualize national ambition, their emission pathways inform scenario analysis, and their methodology demonstrates how to translate emissions data into temperature outcomes — the ultimate measure of climate action adequacy.

## Key Reports / Products
- **Country assessments** — Detailed climate policy evaluations for 40+ countries covering targets, policies, and fair share analysis
- **Global update** — Aggregate warming projection updated around each COP conference
- **CAT Thermometer** — Iconic visualization showing projected warming under different policy scenarios
- **Scaling Up Climate Action series** — Sector-specific analysis of decarbonization potential
- **Climate Target Update Tracker** — Real-time monitoring of NDC submissions and updates

## Data Access
- Country assessments freely available on climateactiontracker.org
- Data underlying projections downloadable for key indicators
- No formal API; data accessed through website and PDF downloads
- Methodology documentation publicly available
- Creative Commons licensing on selected outputs
