# Carbon Tracker

## Overview
Carbon Tracker Initiative is a financial think tank that analyses the impact of the energy transition on capital markets. Founded in 2011, they coined the concept of "stranded assets" — fossil fuel reserves that may become unburnable under climate constraints. Their analysis bridges climate science and financial risk, influencing institutional investors managing trillions in assets to reassess fossil fuel exposure.

## Signature Visual Style
- **Color palette**: Corporate financial style with climate urgency. Dark blues (#1A237E) and greys for base, amber/orange (#FF6F00) for risk highlights, green for transition opportunities. Red for stranded/at-risk categories
- **Typography**: Professional sans-serif, slide-deck-ready. Clear hierarchy with bold metric callouts. Financial table formatting with aligned decimal numerals
- **Layout**: Report-centric with executive summary charts. Bubble charts and cost curves as signature formats. Clean, investor-presentation-ready styling
- **Tone**: Financially rigorous, deliberately speaking the language of investors rather than environmentalists. Risk-focused, not moralizing

## Strengths
- Invented the stranded assets framing that transformed how financial markets view fossil fuel risk
- Cost curve analysis that maps every fossil fuel project against break-even prices and carbon budgets
- Credibility with institutional investors due to financial (not environmental) framing
- Demand destruction scenario modelling showing when fossil fuels become uneconomic
- Effective bridge between climate science and capital allocation decisions

## Chart Types They Favor
- [[Bubble Chart]] — stranded asset exposure by company/country, sized by reserves value, colored by risk level
- [[Line Chart]] — fossil fuel demand decline curves under various transition scenarios
- [[Waterfall Chart]] — cost structure decomposition showing break-even prices for extraction projects
- [[Bar Chart]] — country-level exposure to stranded assets, ranked by financial risk

## Design Patterns Worth Studying
- **Stranded asset valuation**: Bubble charts mapping fossil fuel companies by reserves value vs carbon budget compatibility, revealing which assets cannot be burned
- **Cost curve analysis**: Merit-order-style curves ranking extraction projects from cheapest to most expensive, with carbon budget cutoff line showing which projects are "above the line"
- **Demand destruction scenarios**: Line charts showing fossil fuel demand under different transition speeds, with crossover points where alternatives become cheaper highlighted
- **Financial risk framing**: Presenting climate data in financial terms (NPV at risk, write-down exposure, capex at risk) rather than environmental terms

## Energy & Climate Relevance
Carbon Tracker is directly relevant to understanding the financial dimension of the energy transition. Their stranded asset analysis informs investment decisions, their cost curves reveal which fossil fuel projects are most at risk, and their demand scenarios help organizations assess transition risk in their supply chains — critical context for Scope 3 emissions strategy.

## Key Reports / Products
- **Carbon Bubble** — Foundational report that introduced stranded assets concept to financial markets
- **Decline and Fall** — Analysis of when fossil fuel demand peaks and declines under transition scenarios
- **2 Degrees of Separation** — Assessment of oil and gas company capex alignment with Paris targets
- **Powering Down Coal** — Country-by-country analysis of coal plant economics and retirement timelines
- **Global Registry of Fossil Fuels** — Comprehensive database of fossil fuel production and reserves by country and project

## Data Access
- Reports and summary data freely available on carbontracker.org
- Global Registry of Fossil Fuels provides searchable project-level data
- No formal API for most datasets
- Selected datasets available as downloadable spreadsheets with reports
- Methodology documentation included in all major reports
