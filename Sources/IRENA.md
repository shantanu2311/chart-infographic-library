# IRENA

## Overview
The International Renewable Energy Agency is an intergovernmental organization supporting countries in their transition to sustainable energy. Based in Abu Dhabi, IRENA serves 168 member states with definitive data on renewable energy costs, capacity, and employment. Their LCOE datasets are the global benchmark for renewable energy economics.

## Signature Visual Style
- **Color palette**: Clean blues (#0072BC) and greens (#39B54A) reflecting the renewable energy theme. Technology-specific encoding: solar PV=yellow/amber, onshore wind=green, offshore wind=dark blue, hydro=light blue
- **Typography**: Institutional sans-serif, clear hierarchy between section heads and body, generous white space
- **Layout**: Report-oriented with standalone chart pages. Infographic summaries for social media. Dashboard portals for data exploration
- **Tone**: Optimistic, forward-looking, diplomatically inclusive of developing nations

## Strengths
- Definitive global renewable energy cost data (LCOE by technology, country, and year)
- Comprehensive installed capacity tracking across all renewable technologies
- Strong focus on socioeconomic dimensions (jobs, GDP, welfare) alongside technical data
- Bridges developed and developing country perspectives effectively
- Excellent long-run time series showing dramatic cost decline trajectories

## Chart Types They Favor
- [[Stacked Bar Chart]] — renewable capacity additions by technology and region, year-over-year
- [[Line Chart]] — LCOE cost decline trends, the signature IRENA visualization showing solar/wind learning curves
- [[Choropleth Map]] — renewable energy potential, installed capacity, or policy landscape by country
- [[Area Chart]] — cumulative capacity growth showing the acceleration of renewable deployment

## Design Patterns Worth Studying
- **Cost decline curves**: Plotting LCOE over time with the fossil fuel cost range shown as a horizontal band, visually demonstrating grid parity crossover
- **Capacity factor comparisons**: Side-by-side bar charts comparing capacity factors across technologies and regions
- **Job creation infographics**: Pictogram-style displays of renewable energy employment by sector and country
- **Investment gap analysis**: Comparing current investment levels against what is needed for 1.5C pathways

## Energy & Climate Relevance
IRENA's cost data directly supports business cases for renewable energy investment, which is central to Scope 2 emissions reduction strategies. Their capacity and generation data feeds into grid emission factor calculations, and their transition pathways inform corporate science-based targets.

## Key Reports / Products
- **Renewable Energy Statistics** — Annual comprehensive dataset: capacity, generation, and trade flows for all renewables
- **World Energy Transitions Outlook (WETO)** — Flagship scenario report: 1.5C-aligned transition pathway to 2050
- **Renewable Power Generation Costs** — Annual LCOE benchmarking across solar, wind, hydro, bioenergy, geothermal
- **Renewable Energy and Jobs** — Annual review of global renewable energy employment
- **REmap** — Country-level renewable energy roadmaps and potential assessments

## Data Access
- Open download portal with CSV/Excel files for all major datasets
- Interactive data explorer (IRENA RE Statistics) with country and technology filters
- No API currently; bulk downloads organized by report and year
- Data freely available for research and non-commercial use
- Query tool for custom cross-tabulations of capacity and generation data
