# List Infographic

## What It Is
A numbered or ranked infographic format that presents information as an ordered sequence of items, each with a visual anchor, headline, and supporting detail. It combines the scanability of a listicle with the visual impact of an infographic, making it the most accessible format for broad audiences. The implicit ranking — whether by importance, magnitude, or sequence — gives readers a built-in prioritization framework without requiring them to interpret complex charts.

## Key Characteristics
- Numbered or ranked items with clear visual ordering (1, 2, 3... or largest to smallest)
- Consistent card or row template repeated for each item
- Icon or small illustration per item for visual differentiation
- Brief headline + 1-2 sentence explanation per item
- Optional quantitative indicator (bar segment, percentage badge, or metric callout) per item

## Best Use Cases
- Top 10 global emitters by total CO2e or per-capita emissions
- Key outcomes and decisions from COP summits
- Investment trends: largest clean energy deals, top funded technologies, or leading ESG funds
- Policy recommendations from IPCC Working Group reports or national climate plans
- Technology Readiness Level (TRL) rankings for emerging clean technologies
- Funding scheme eligibility checklists for MSMEs

### Energy & Climate Applications
- Top 10 countries by installed renewable energy capacity with year-over-year growth
- Five key pledges from the latest COP presidency covering mitigation, adaptation, and finance
- Ranking of Scope 3 categories by typical contribution to total corporate footprint
- Seven eligibility criteria for SIDBI or ADEETIE funding schemes for Indian MSMEs
- Ten most cost-effective emission reduction technologies for the iron and steel sector
- Priority actions for net-zero pathways ranked by abatement cost per tCO2e

## Structure & Layout
A vertical stack of 5-10 item cards, each occupying a consistent height band. Each card contains: a rank number or icon on the left, a headline in bold, a supporting metric or data point (often as a badge or inline bar), and 1-2 sentences of context. Alternating background shading or subtle divider lines separate items. The top item receives slightly more visual emphasis (larger number, accent color, or expanded detail). A header establishes the ranking criterion and data source. A footer provides methodology notes and the date of the underlying data. For ranked lists, a small horizontal bar or lollipop extending from each item shows the quantitative basis for the ranking.

## Design Tips
> [!tip] Best Practices
> - Cap the list at 10 items maximum; "Top 7" or "5 Key Takeaways" performs better than "Top 25" in static formats
> - Make the ranking criterion explicit in the title (e.g., "by installed GW" or "by abatement cost")
> - Use progressive color intensity — the top-ranked item gets the deepest hue, fading as rank decreases
> - Include a small inline chart (mini bar or sparkline) per item to ground the ranking in data
> - Add a "Why it matters" sentence for each item to move beyond raw ranking into insight

## When NOT to Use
> [!warning] Avoid When
> - Items are not meaningfully ranked or ordered — an unordered set of facts is better as a statistical infographic
> - The audience needs to compare items on multiple dimensions simultaneously (use a comparison infographic)
> - The list exceeds 12 items and each requires significant detail (use an interactive table or dashboard)

## Recommended Chart Types
- [[Bar Chart]] — horizontal bars extending from each list item to show the quantitative ranking basis
- [[Lollipop Chart]] — cleaner alternative to bars for ranked lists, with a dot at the value endpoint
- [[Dot Plot]] — showing each item's position along a single quantitative axis with labeled points
