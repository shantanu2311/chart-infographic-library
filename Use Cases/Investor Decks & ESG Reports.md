# Investor Decks & ESG Reports

## Overview
Investor decks and ESG reports present climate and sustainability data to financial decision-makers who allocate capital based on risk-adjusted returns. These visualizations must balance data density with narrative clarity, demonstrating both current performance and forward-looking resilience. Regulatory frameworks like TCFD, ISSB, and EU Taxonomy increasingly prescribe specific disclosure formats that shape visualization requirements.

## Audience
- Institutional investors and portfolio managers
- ESG analysts and rating agency professionals
- Fund managers (green bonds, climate funds, impact investing)
- Corporate board members and CFOs reviewing sustainability performance
- Regulatory bodies (SEC, SEBI, ESMA) reviewing climate disclosures

## Key Requirements
- Professional aesthetic consistent with financial industry presentation standards
- Forward-looking scenario analysis (TCFD-aligned physical and transition risk)
- Regulatory alignment with ISSB S2, EU CSRD, BRSR, and SEC climate rule formats
- Drill-down capability from portfolio-level to asset-level exposure data
- Consistent time-series benchmarking against indices, peers, and targets

## Recommended Chart Types
- [[Treemap Chart]] — Visualizing portfolio carbon exposure by sector, geography, or asset class proportionally
- [[Radar Chart]] — Comparing multi-dimensional ESG scores across environmental, social, and governance pillars
- [[Heatmap]] — Displaying climate risk matrices (likelihood vs. impact) or sector transition readiness
- [[Candlestick Chart]] — Showing carbon credit price movements, green bond yield spreads, and market volatility
- [[Waterfall Chart]] — Decomposing financed emissions changes from base year through interventions to target

## Recommended Visual Styles
- [[Corporate]] — Polished, branded presentations with consistent color systems that signal institutional credibility
- [[Dark Mode]] — High-contrast dark backgrounds favored in financial terminals and boardroom presentations for visual impact

## Recommended Infographic Formats
- [[Statistical Infographic]] — KPI-dense layouts with multiple metrics, sparklines, and delta indicators for quarterly reporting
- [[Comparison Infographic]] — Side-by-side fund performance, portfolio carbon intensity benchmarking, and peer analysis

## Example Outputs
- TCFD climate risk scenario analysis with physical and transition risk overlays
- Green bond impact report showing proceeds allocation and environmental outcomes
- Climate fund quarterly factsheet with carbon metrics, attribution, and NAV
- Sovereign ESG country risk briefing with macro indicators and climate vulnerability
- Portfolio decarbonization glide path tracking actual vs. Paris-aligned trajectory

### Energy & Climate Examples
- Stranded asset risk assessment across fossil fuel holdings with write-down scenarios
- Scope 1/2/3 financed emissions waterfall by sector with year-on-year attribution
- Climate VaR heatmap across geographies under RCP 4.5 and RCP 8.5 scenarios
- Renewable energy investment returns compared to fossil fuel benchmarks over 10 years
- Carbon price sensitivity analysis showing portfolio impact at $50, $100, $150/tCO2

## Tools & Platforms
- Bloomberg Terminal for real-time financial and ESG data visualization
- Tableau or Power BI for interactive dashboard creation with drill-down capability
- Pitch or Google Slides with custom templates for investor presentation decks
- MSCI ESG Manager or Sustainalytics for standardized ESG scoring visualizations

## Design Tips
> [!tip] Best Practices
> - Use consistent color coding for scenarios across all figures (e.g., blue=current, green=target, red=risk)
> - Include materiality context: show why a metric matters, not just what the number is
> - Provide clear year-on-year deltas with directional arrows for KPI dashboards
> - Design for both projected display (16:9) and printed handout (A4) simultaneously
> - Add regulatory framework badges (TCFD, ISSB, EU Taxonomy) to signal compliance alignment

## Common Mistakes
> [!warning] Avoid These
> - Cherry-picking favorable time periods that misrepresent long-term performance trends
> - Using pie charts for portfolio composition where treemaps show hierarchy more effectively
> - Presenting absolute emissions without normalizing by revenue, AUM, or production output
> - Burying methodology assumptions in appendices instead of annotating directly on figures
