# Business Reports & Dashboards

## Overview
Business reports and dashboards serve internal corporate decision-makers who need to monitor sustainability KPIs, track emissions reduction progress, and report to boards and regulators. These visualizations must be consistent, drill-down capable, and connected to live or frequently updated data sources. The emphasis is on actionable insight: not just what happened, but what to do next.

## Audience
- Chief Sustainability Officers and ESG teams
- Board members and C-suite executives reviewing quarterly performance
- Operations managers tracking facility-level energy and emissions metrics
- Procurement teams monitoring supply chain sustainability scores
- Internal audit and compliance teams verifying reporting accuracy

## Key Requirements
- KPI-focused design with clear targets, actuals, and variance indicators
- Consistent branding aligned with corporate visual identity guidelines
- Drill-down capability from portfolio level to facility level to source level
- Real-time or near-real-time data refresh for operational dashboards
- Export capability to PDF, PowerPoint, and Excel for board reporting workflows

## Recommended Chart Types
- [[Bullet Chart]] — Comparing actual performance against targets and benchmarks in a compact, information-dense format
- [[Heatmap]] — Identifying patterns across facilities, time periods, or emission categories with color intensity encoding
- [[Line Chart]] — Tracking KPI trends over time with target lines, forecast bands, and anomaly highlighting
- [[Stacked Bar Chart]] — Breaking down emissions by scope, facility, or source category with period-over-period comparison
- [[Waterfall Chart]] — Explaining the bridge from last period's emissions to current period through specific drivers and interventions

## Recommended Visual Styles
- [[Corporate]] — Clean, branded layouts with company colors, consistent typography, and professional formatting for board consumption
- [[Dashboard]] — Dense, multi-panel layouts optimized for at-a-glance monitoring with sparklines, gauges, and status indicators

## Recommended Infographic Formats
- [[Statistical Infographic]] — Executive summary pages with hero KPIs, trend arrows, and contextual benchmarks for board packs
- [[Comparison Infographic]] — Facility benchmarking, peer comparison, and year-on-year performance delta summaries

## Example Outputs
- Corporate carbon footprint dashboard with Scope 1/2/3 breakdown and monthly tracking
- ESG KPI scorecard with RAG (red-amber-green) status indicators for board reporting
- Energy management system interface showing real-time consumption by facility and equipment
- Procurement sustainability scoring dashboard ranking suppliers by carbon intensity
- BRSR Principle 6 compliance tracker mapping data availability against required disclosures

### Energy & Climate Examples
- Facility-level energy intensity heatmap across months with weather normalization
- Scope 2 market-based vs. location-based emissions comparison with REC retirement tracking
- Fleet electrification progress dashboard showing ICE vs. EV split and charging infrastructure
- Carbon offset portfolio tracker with retirement schedule, vintage, and registry verification
- Science-based target progress chart showing actual trajectory vs. 1.5C pathway glide path

## Tools & Platforms
- Power BI for enterprise-grade dashboards with row-level security and scheduled refresh
- Tableau for interactive exploration with parameter controls and dashboard actions
- Grafana for real-time operational monitoring connected to IoT and SCADA data sources
- Looker or Metabase for SQL-based analytical dashboards with embedded sharing

## Design Tips
> [!tip] Best Practices
> - Place the most critical KPIs in the top-left quadrant where eyes land first (F-pattern reading)
> - Use conditional formatting (RAG colors) sparingly and consistently: red=off track, amber=at risk, green=on target
> - Design for the "30-second board glance": if the key message is not obvious in half a minute, simplify
> - Include comparison context on every metric (vs. target, vs. last year, vs. benchmark)
> - Build filter hierarchies (company > business unit > facility > source) for progressive drill-down

## Common Mistakes
> [!warning] Avoid These
> - Displaying too many KPIs on a single screen, creating visual noise instead of clarity
> - Using 3D charts or decorative elements that reduce data-ink ratio and slow comprehension
> - Failing to distinguish between operational dashboards (real-time, action-oriented) and reporting dashboards (periodic, summary)
> - Hardcoding date ranges instead of building dynamic rolling windows and comparison periods
