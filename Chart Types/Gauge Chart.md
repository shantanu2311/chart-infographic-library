# Gauge Chart

## What It Is
A gauge chart displays a single metric on a semi-circular or circular dial, resembling a speedometer. The needle or filled arc indicates the current value relative to a defined minimum and maximum range. Color-coded zones (green/yellow/red) communicate whether performance is on target, at risk, or critical.

## Best Data Types
- Single numeric value against a known target or range
- Percentages and completion rates
- KPIs with defined thresholds (good/warning/danger)
- Ratios and scores bounded between fixed limits
- Real-time or periodically updated metrics

## When to Use
- Displaying a single KPI at a glance on a dashboard
- Showing progress toward a target (e.g., 72% of NDC commitment achieved)
- Communicating whether a metric falls in an acceptable, warning, or critical zone
- Executive summaries where one number tells the story
- Operational dashboards requiring instant visual status

## When NOT to Use
> [!warning] Avoid When
> - You need to compare multiple metrics side by side (use [[Bullet Chart]] instead)
> - The metric has no meaningful upper/lower bound
> - You need to show trends over time (use [[Line Chart]] or [[Sparkline]])
> - Space is limited and you have many KPIs (use [[Waffle Chart]] or simple numbers)
> - The audience needs precision rather than approximate position

## Real-World Use Cases
- Vehicle dashboard speedometers and fuel gauges
- Server CPU/memory utilization in IT monitoring dashboards
- Customer satisfaction score displays
- Project completion percentage in PMO dashboards

### Energy & Climate Applications
- Percentage of Nationally Determined Contribution (NDC) target achieved
- Renewable energy share in total generation mix (gauge showing 0-100%)
- Emission intensity vs. sector benchmark (green if below, red if above)
- Carbon budget remaining as a percentage of total allocation
- ESG composite score dial for corporate sustainability reporting
- Grid frequency deviation gauge for power system operators

## Visual Style Variations
- **Half-circle gauge** — classic speedometer arc from left to right
- **Full-circle gauge** — 360-degree ring with filled portion indicating value
- **Multi-zone gauge** — concentric rings showing multiple related KPIs
- **Minimalist arc** — thin arc with large centered number, no needle

## Related Charts
- [[Bullet Chart]] — linear alternative that supports comparison against targets and benchmarks more compactly
- [[Donut Chart]] — ring chart showing single-value completion; similar visual weight
- [[Waffle Chart]] — grid-based percentage display; better for side-by-side KPI comparison
- [[Sparkline]] — inline trend context that a gauge lacks
