# Waffle Chart

## What It Is
A waffle chart is a grid of small squares (typically 10x10 = 100 squares) where each square represents 1% of the total. Squares are filled with different colors to show proportional composition. It offers a more perceptually accurate alternative to the [[Pie Chart]] because humans are better at counting discrete units than judging angles or areas. Waffle charts are highly effective in infographics and public-facing communications.

## Best Data Types
- Percentages and proportions (ideally summing to 100%)
- Binary splits (e.g., 72% renewable vs 28% fossil)
- Small-count proportions where each unit is meaningful (e.g., 17 out of 20 countries)
- Comparative proportions across 2-3 categories

## When to Use
- Making percentage breakdowns intuitively graspable for general audiences
- Infographics, policy briefs, and media publications where visual clarity is paramount
- Comparing proportions side-by-side using multiple waffle grids (small multiples)
- Highlighting progress toward a target (e.g., 43% of NDCs on track)
- When you want to avoid the perceptual distortions of pie charts

## When NOT to Use
> [!warning] Avoid When
> - You have more than 4-5 categories (the grid becomes a patchwork of too many colors)
> - Values do not round neatly to whole percentages (0.3% is hard to represent in 100 squares)
> - You need to show hierarchy or nested composition (use [[Treemap]] or [[Sunburst Chart]])
> - Time-series comparison is needed (use [[Stacked Bar Chart]] or area charts)
> - Precise decimal percentages must be communicated

## Real-World Use Cases
- Election results: percentage of seats won by each party
- Survey results: proportion of respondents by answer category
- Health statistics: percentage of population vaccinated
- Fundraising: progress toward a donation goal

### Energy & Climate Applications
- Percentage of renewable energy in a country's electricity mix (e.g., 38 of 100 squares filled green)
- Share of global climate finance reaching the most vulnerable nations vs rest of world
- Percentage of countries with NDCs on track to meet Paris Agreement temperature targets
- Proportion of global energy investment going to clean energy vs fossil fuels (IEA WEI)
- Share of Scope 3 emissions in a company's total carbon footprint

## Visual Style Variations
- **Standard 10x10 grid** — 100 squares, each representing 1%, color-coded by category
- **Icon waffle** — replaces squares with thematic icons (e.g., solar panels, smokestacks)
- **Small multiples waffle** — side-by-side grids comparing multiple entities (e.g., countries)
- **Isotype waffle** — each icon represents a fixed unit count (e.g., 1 icon = 10 MW installed)

## Related Charts
- [[Pie Chart]] — the classic alternative; waffle charts are generally more perceptually accurate
- [[Donut Chart]] — ring-based alternative with center annotation capability
- [[Stacked Bar Chart]] — better for comparing proportions across many groups or over time
